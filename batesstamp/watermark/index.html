About PDF Watermarking

What is PDF Watermarking?

PDF watermarking applies visible text overlays to document pages to indicate their status or sensitivity level. Legal professionals commonly use watermarks like CONFIDENTIAL, DRAFT, or ATTORNEY EYES ONLY to clearly communicate a document's handling requirements to all recipients.

Common Watermark Uses in Legal Practice

  • CONFIDENTIAL: Mark documents subject to confidentiality agreements or protective orders
  • DRAFT: Distinguish working versions from final executed documents
  • ATTORNEY EYES ONLY: Restrict sensitive discovery materials to counsel only
  • PRIVILEGED: Flag attorney-client privileged communications
  • DO NOT DISTRIBUTE: Limit circulation of sensitive documents
  • COPY: Identify document copies vs. originals
